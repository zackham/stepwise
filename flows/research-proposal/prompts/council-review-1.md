## Review criteria

Address each of the following:

1. **Research question clarity** — Is the research question or proposal objective well-defined and grounded? Is the scope appropriate?

2. **Evidence quality** — Are claims supported by evidence from the codebase, documentation, or external sources? Are references specific and verifiable?

3. **Gaps and blind spots** — What's missing? What assumptions are unstated? What important aspects of the topic are not addressed?

4. **Technical viability** — Is the proposed approach viable given the constraints? Are there implementation challenges that are underestimated?

5. **Alternatives** — Are the alternatives considered reasonable? Are there obvious alternatives that were missed?

6. **Risks** — Are the identified risks realistic? Are there risks that were overlooked?

Be specific and constructive. Reference particular sections, claims, or gaps. Don't just say something is "good" or "bad" — explain why and suggest improvements.
